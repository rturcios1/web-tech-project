  • Cascada Los Tercios
    • These waterfalls are particularly cool because of the geometric shape of the rocks. The best time to visit is during the winter months of MONTHS. This waterfall is only 5-10 minutes away from the town and accessible by foot, car, or moto-taxi.
  • Museo de Alejandro Cotto
    • Salvadoran filmmaker Alejandro Cotto passed away in YEAR but his legacy lives on through his works and his house which now operates as a museum. All the furniture is vintage and the architecture is that of a traditional wealthy Salvadoran home. There are also a number of gardens and my personal favorite is the one that looks out to the Lago de Suchitlan
  • Centro Arte para la Paz
    • This is an art school but they have several galleries with historical information about the town and exhibits of students’ work. This is another great place to see the talent of those that live in Suchitoto
  • Ruinas de Cihuatan
    • These ruins aren’t in Suchitoto, but they are close enough that I recommend visiting both places on the same day. Personally, I think these ruins have been preserved better and more authentically than la Joya de Ceren and las Ruinas de San Andrés. The one hour drive is worth it!

My Experience

I love visiting Suchitoto because of the old-school colorful houses. Slowly, it’s becoming rare to see traditional houses like the ones in Suchitoto. Because of this, the mayor of the town has set certain rules regarding house remodels. I also love going on boat rides near the man-made lake that helps generate the PERCENTAGE of El Salvador’s sustainable energy. When in Suchitoto there are also a number of craft workshops. The most popular workshop is with indigo. For some time, El Salvador was one of the main exporters of organic indigo and one of the biggest indigo farms are based in Suchitoto. Not only do they grow and cultivate the product, but they also host workshops to tie-dye fabrics using indigo.
