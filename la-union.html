Things to See

  • Mirador Espiritu de la Montana
    • This is a must visit in El Salvador! While you can get a good look of the Gulf of Fonseca during the day, I highly reccomend spending the night to catch the sunrise. This is a breathtaking experience and there are several other activities to do on the camping grounds. Also, it's super affordable.
  • Parque de La Familia
    • There are several mosaics and sculptures in the area. There is also a lighthouse you can walk to the top of to see the rest of the gulf. From this location, you can actually catch a boat tour to see the rest of the neighboring islands and even visit an island like La Isla del Zacatillo
  • Isla Zacatillo
    • There are several Salvadoran islands in the Gulf of Fonseca including La Isla del Zacatillo which is the only one I’ve been to. Overall, my experience at this island was great, and I went when they were just starting to develop tourism in this sector. The beach area is much better than other beaches because the waves are less aggressive.

My Experience

I didn’t realize how much el Oriente de El Salvador had to offer until I visited La Union a few years back. Before coming to this realization I spent most of my time in San Salvador, the capital, and La Libertad, the beaches closest to the capital. As much as I love the new places I have discovered the Mirador Espiritu de la Montana has been my favorite by far. There are tons of hiking trails, grilling sites, dogs to play with, and camping grounds. If you visit Conchagua, you need to camp to catch the sunrise of the Gulf of Fonseca
