Things to See

  • Laguna de Alegria
    • The lagoon is the town’s greatest attraction. The high levels of sulfur in the water of this volcanic crater make the lagoon a bright green color. It’s only 5 minutes in a car from the center of the town, but you can also walk to the lagoon or take a tuk-tuk, which is actually called moto-taxis in El Salvador
  • Mirador de las Cien Gradas
    • They recently re-painted these stairs so they have a rainbow pattern. It’s a great place to visit to take photos of the landscape. In fact, you can even see el Rio Lempa from the top of these stairs. At the top of the stairs, there are also several traditional food vendors.
  • Aparicion de Maria
    • This is actually a town secret. While most people know about the legend of the siren that attracts men to the lagoon, outsiders don’t know about the supposed apparition of the Virgin Mary in this colorful town. After walking down the Mirador de las Cien Gradas, keep walking to the right and you will most likely run into it. I recommend you ask a local for more information and they will most likely call over the local that has dedicated their time to sharing information about this space and the “miracles” that have taken place. Even if you’re not religious, it’s a cool experience to see and hear about

Best Restaurants

I actually don’t dine in at restaurants in Alegria. Instead, I’ll visit the street vendors to try a few of my favorite snacks, but if I were to recommend places I say you have to check out. The food at the restaurants is on par with what you could get from the vendors but the views and ambiance of the restaurants are nicer. My Experience

Alegria is a must-visit for my family and me. It’s a simple town with charm. We like to go every summer because it’s close to one of my parent’s hometowns, and due to the high altitude, it’s a nice get away from the city heat and commotion. On the weekdays, the town does feel a little boring because there aren’t many people out and about. On the weekends, it’s a completely different story. A large number of vendors (food and artisanal craft) gather at the main plaza on weekends to sell. If you’re only visiting El Salvador for a few days, I recommend using Alegria as your pitstop between La Libertad.and Perquin or La Union.Although El Salvador is a small country sometimes there can be a lot of traffic a 3-4 hour drives turns into longer especially if there is a construction underway, which is the case in 2021. Alegria is a nice break and has a lot of traditional architecture which resemble that of Antigua Guatemala and Suchitoto
