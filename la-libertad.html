Things to See

  • Tamanique Waterfalls
    • There are 4 waterfalls on this trail. Some are more challenging to get to than others, but at least at the end of the trial there is the reward of taking a swim in the refreshing waters. The journey from El Tamanique to the first waterfall is approximately 40 minutes, the other waterfalls are between 10-20 minutes away from the first.
  • Professional Surfers in Action
    • They hold several surfing competitions in the area which are super fun to watch! I also recommend taking a few surfing lessons while you’re in the area. The only thing is that the beaches in this area have a lot of rocks at the base instead of sand, so it can be a rough experience for beginners or to even relax at.

My Experience

I like La Libertad because there is always something to do. Despite what you hear on the news regarding the dangers of visiting El Salvador, La Libertad has been able to prosper. Surfers’ attraction of intense waves for training and competition has created an economic boom to allow for this specific region of El Salvador to develop. Because of the constant tourism, there are a ton of restaurants and transportation methods that make it easy for a tourist to make the most out of their trip. Whether I only go for the day or stay the night, pre-covid times, I was always able to find a place with a lively environment.
